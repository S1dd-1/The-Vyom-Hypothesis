# The-Vyom-Hypothesis

## Abstract

The universe came from nothing, and thus, it must return to nothing. While modern physics, including quantum mechanics and general relativity, has made significant progress in explaining how systems behave, this paper offers a speculative framework to explore why systems behave the way they do. It introduces the postulate that all systems follow a singular principle: the tendency to become non-existent. By defining existence as a function of a system’s physical, electromagnetic, and quantum complexity, this hypothesis aims to provide insight into the nature of fundamental forces, the role of dark energy, and the possibility of a fractal universe structure. Ultimately, the theory aspires to be a step towards the theory of everything.
